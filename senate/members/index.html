Aaron Finkel - - Class of 2020
asfinkel@brandeis.edu

Aaron Finkel is the 2016-2017 Massell Quad Senator. He’s from sunny Miami, FL, and expects to be studying either politics, philosophy, English, music, or all four! (Why not? It's Brandeis!) After his undergraduate years, he plans to attend law school. Aaron is both thrilled to be a member of such an outstanding community, and humbled by the opportunity to serve the student body. Brandeis is entering an exciting new era. The school has new administrators, new campus facilities, new and ambitious plans for the future, and at the same time, new challenges. Brandeisians all come with innovative ideas and perspectives, and it’s the role of the Student Union to make their dreams a reality. Throughout his time in the Union, Aaron will do his best to address the issues that are important to the student body, especially to students living within Massell’s four residence halls. Come see him in his office hours, email him, or write to him on social media. Aaron is committed to being as open and transparent as possible in his role as Senator, and is always looking for suggestions from his constituents.

Geraldine Bogard - - Class of 2020
gbogard@brandeis.edu

Geraldine Bogard was born and raised in Haiti. Three years ago she moved to the United States and started her pursuit of becoming a leader to bring social change to her community. At high school, she was a change agent in the Mayor’s Youth Council (MYC) in Boston. MYC is a group of high school representatives from the neighborhoods of Boston that join together to advocate for change in their communities. Advocating for other and helping people, especially the people who do not feel like they have a voice, is a passion of Geraldine’s. This year, Geraldine is in her first year at Brandeis and is part of the Myra Kraft Transitional Program (MKTYP). She was elected as a senator for the MKTYP program. She is looking forward to listening and advocating for her constituents, and most importantly, creating an avenue for them to bring their voice to what is occurring on campus. Apart from the Student Union, Geraldine is a member of the Christian bible study group, Black African Student Organization (BASO), Platinum Step Team and the Vice-President of Recognition for North Quad Community Council.

Hannah Brown - - Class of 2019
hcbrown@brandeis.edu

Hannah Brown is sophomore majoring in economics and international and global studies. She was born and raised in Longview, Texas, but she doesn't have a Southern accent so it may be hard to tell. This her second year in the Senate, and she is the current Executive Senator, Senator for the Class of 2019, and Chair of the Senate Services and Outreach Committee. Last year, she served as the Senator for North Quad and the Dining Committee Chair. This year, Hannah aims to make Brandeis a more cohesive, supportive, happier, healthier, and safer place through extensive outreach and projects. She is always here as a resource and loves getting to know people, so please do not hesitate to reach out to her. Outside of the Union, she is a Project Manager in TAMID and a Global Fellow. In her free time, she enjoys exercising, reading, writing, and spending time with her friends and family.

Lian Chen - - Class of 2019
lianc@brandeis.edu

Lian is very excited to be your representative of Racial Minority group! Lian comes form Shanghai, China. She majors in Mathematics and Economics, and minor in Sculpture. She serves as the treasure of Swing Dance Club and is an instructor of Brandeis Pottery Club. She joined Student Union because She loves Brandeis and want to make it a place that every other student loves, too. As a new Senate member, she is passionate about her job as a Senator and a member of Club Support Committee and Service and Outreach Committee and will fully committed in helping clubs and holding meaningful events on campus. All your voices are very important to her, and she would love to talk with any of you to hear your concerns and know about your suggestions about school. And she will make sure to take actions on the things you hope to change. She would especially work hard on making sure that voices of people, who are fro minority groups, will be heard. She is patient and very approachable, so please do not hesitate to contact her through lianc@brandies.edu, directly talk to her, or come to her office hour in Student Union Office (SCC 301) on Thursday from 12pm to 3pm.
