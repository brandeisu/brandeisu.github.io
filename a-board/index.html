Allocations Board

The Allocations Board is comprised of 11 voting members: a chairperson, the Student Union Treasurer, a Senate Representative, two racial minority members, and six other members. It is advised by Student Activities. It holds meetings at least once per week during the semester.

As outlined in Article V of the Student Union Constitution, A-Boardâ€™s role is to establish the budgets from the Student Activities Fund (SAF) for Chartered and Secured Clubs. SAF funding is taken as 1% of the Brandeis University tuition and for the 2017-2018 academic year is approximately $1.7 million.

Only secured and chartered organizations are eligible to request and receive funds from A-Board. The Student Union Senate recognizes and charters all clubs.

A-Board strives to ensure that all funding is distributed equitably and will only fund requests that are relevant to a club's constitution, within the budgetary means of the SAF, and meet the requirements of A-Board policy.

To meet with Allocations Board please check the office hours.

Allocations Board Values

As elected representatives of the student body, our values must reflect those of the undergraduate population. To us this means:

  • Personal biases are recognized and mitigated
  • Accessibility and transparency are key to our processes
  • Members remain involved in and informed on campus culture
  • Student feedback is meaningfully incorporated
  • Allocations Board policies encourage innovation

As stewards of funds, we must responsibly and efficiently allocate funding. To us this means:

  • Funding decisions are consistent and predictable
  • All dealings with the Allocations Board are professional
  • We collaborate effectively with the Student Union Treasury
  • All semesterly goals fit within a long term fiscal vision
  • Our funding structure is robust and reliable
