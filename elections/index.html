Elections

Be the change you want to see in Brandeis

There are typically three sets of elections every academic year. Spring Elections are held for terms that take place the following academic year. Additionally, the Spring Elections are divided into two rounds; one for executive positions and one for Senate positions. Occasionally, the Union will also hold Special Elections to fill seats in special circumstances. To see what elections happen when, check the outline below.

Fall Elections

Fall Elections are held at the beginning of the Fall Semester, usually around late August and early September. Positions available for election include the following:

  • Quad Senator Positions
  • Allocations Board Members

Winter Elections

Winter Elections are held at the beginning of the Spring Semester, usually in mid-to-late January. Positions available for election include the following:

  • Allocations Board Members
  • Midyear Senator
  • Special Elections for seats vacated between semesters

Spring Elections

Spring Elections are held near the end of the Spring Semester, usually in late March and early April. Elections are also broken up into two rounds. Positions available for election include the following:

Spring First Round (for subsequent academic year)

  • President
  • Vice President
  • Treasurer
  • Secretary
  • Junior Representative to the Board of Trustees
  • Representative to the Undergraduate Curriculum Committee
  • Representative to the Brandeis Sustainability Fund

Spring Second Round (for subsequent academic year)

  • Class Year Senator Positions
  • Senators at Large
  • Judiciary Seats
  • Uncontested or undecided elections from the first round.

The next round of elections will be held in Fall 2018.

For Information on the Exact Dates, please contact Qingtian Mei, the Secretary of the Student Union.
